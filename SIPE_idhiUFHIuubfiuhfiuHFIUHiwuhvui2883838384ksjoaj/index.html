How to get here

Easy connections from Alicante and Corvera airports...

By Plane: The best options are Alicante Airport (ALC), which has a direct bus to Murcia (takes about 55 minutes), or Corvera Airport (RMU), which is closer but has fewer international flights.

By Train: The AVE (high-speed train) connects Madrid directly with Murcia in just 2 hours and 45 minutes.

Gastronomy

You can't leave without trying a 'marinera'...

La Marinera: A crunchy breadstick topped with Russian salad and an anchovy. The ultimate Murcian tapa.

Paparajotes: A traditional dessert made by deep-frying a lemon leaf coated in dough and sugar. (Pro tip: You don't eat the leaf, just the dough!).

What to visit

Get lost around the Cathedral, visit the Real Casino...

Catedral de Murcia: A mix of Gothic, Renaissance, and Baroque styles. Its tower is the second tallest in Spain.

Real Casino: A stunning 19th-century gentlemen's club with an incredible Moorish courtyard inspired by the Alhambra.

Plaza de las Flores: The absolute heart of the city's social life, perfect for grabbing a cold beer and a tapa after the conferences.

Santuario de la Fuensanta: Located in the mountains overlooking the city, it offers the best panoramic views of the entire valley.
